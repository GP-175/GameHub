# Cube Basher Gameplay Guide

Cube Basher is a 3D survival action game inside GameHub. You choose a hero, run around an open world, avoid a growing horde of enemy cubes, collect gems, level up, pick new powers, and try to survive for 30 minutes.

The most important thing to understand: your hero attacks automatically. Your job is to move well, stay alive, collect rewards, choose smart upgrades, and guide the enemies into your weapons.

## 1. Goal of the Game

Your main goal is to survive until the timer reaches 30:00.

During the run you should:

- Bash as many enemy cubes as possible.
- Collect XP gems to level up.
- Choose weapons and passive upgrades when you level up.
- Pick up gold and health items.
- Defeat elite cubes and bosses when they appear.
- Use coins after a run to buy permanent upgrades in the Upgrade Lab.

You win when you survive the full 30 minutes. You lose when your health reaches 0.

## 2. How to Start

1. Open Cube Basher from GameHub.
2. Pick one of the three characters on the main menu.
3. Press `BASH!` to start the run.
4. Move immediately. Enemies spawn around you and begin chasing you.
5. Collect gems and choose upgrades whenever the level-up screen appears.

You can also open `UPGRADES` from the menu to spend saved coins before starting.

## 4. Basic Gameplay Loop

A run follows this loop:

1. Move through the world while enemies chase you.
2. Your weapons fire automatically when their cooldowns are ready.
3. Defeated enemies drop XP gems and sometimes gold or health.
4. Pick up XP gems to fill the XP bar.
5. When the XP bar fills, choose one of three level-up cards.
6. Keep moving, collecting, and upgrading as the horde gets stronger.
7. Survive bosses and elites.
8. Reach 30:00 to win, or die and use earned coins to improve future runs.

## 5. Main Screens

### Main Menu

The main menu lets you:

- Choose your character.
- Start a run with `BASH!`.
- Open the `UPGRADES` shop.
- See your current saved coin total.
- Return to GameHub.

### In-Game HUD

During a run, the screen shows:

- XP bar at the top.
- Current level badge.
- Timer in the top center.
- Kill count and gold count in the top right.
- Weapon list and passive icons on the left.
- Health and stamina bars at the bottom left.
- Boss health bar when a boss is active.
- Combo counter when you chain enough kills.

### Level-Up Screen

When you level up, the game pauses and shows three cards. Pick one card to continue.

Cards can be:

- A new weapon.
- An upgrade to a weapon you already have.
- A passive stat upgrade.

If you gain multiple levels at once, the screen may show `LEVEL UP! (x2)` or higher. You will choose one card for each pending level.

### Pause Screen

Pause stops the run and the music. You can resume or quit to GameHub.

Important: gold from the current run becomes permanent coins only when the run ends through death or victory. Quitting mid-run may leave that run's gold unbanked.

### End Screens

If you die, the game shows `YOU GOT BASHED`.

If you survive 30 minutes, the game shows `YOU BASHED THE WORLD`.

Both end screens show:

- Survival time.
- Final level.
- Cubes bashed.
- Gems collected.
- Coins banked from the run.
- Total saved coins.

## 6. Characters

Each character starts with different health, speed, and starting weapon.

| Character | Starting weapon | Health | Speed | Best for |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Bonky | Basher Hammer | 100 | 8.0 | Balanced first runs |
| Zippy | Boomerang | 75 | 10.0 | Fast movement and dodging |
| Chonk | Bash Orbs | 160 | 7.0 | Surviving hits and learning enemy behavior |

### Bonky

Bonky is the classic balanced character. Bonky starts with the Basher Hammer, which damages enemies around you. This is a good first character because the stats are simple and forgiving.

### Zippy

Zippy moves much faster but has less health. Zippy starts with the Boomerang, which can hit enemies at range. Zippy rewards active movement and careful dodging.

### Chonk

Chonk has much more health but moves slower. Chonk starts with Bash Orbs, which orbit the player and hit nearby enemies. Chonk is useful for learning because the extra health gives more room for mistakes.

## 7. Movement and Survival

Movement is the main skill in Cube Basher.

Enemies usually move toward you, so standing still is dangerous. You should keep circling, weaving, and leading enemies through your automatic attacks.

### Sprinting

Holding sprint makes you move faster, but it drains stamina.

Stamina rules:

- Sprint uses stamina while you are moving.
- If stamina reaches 0, you become winded.
- While winded, you cannot sprint again until the stamina bar refills completely.
- If you stop sprinting before the bar empties, stamina refills and sprint stays available.

Use sprint in short bursts. Do not hold sprint until empty unless you must escape.

### Jumping

Jumping helps you:

- Hop over enemies.
- Avoid some contact damage.
- Jump over spitter projectiles.
- Move up ledges that cannot be walked up directly.

Jumping is not an attack by itself. It is a positioning and survival tool.

### Terrain and Obstacles

The world has hills, mesas, trees, rocks, flowers, grass, clouds, and a glowing edge boundary.

Important terrain rules:

- Trees and larger rocks are solid obstacles.
- Hills and ledges affect movement.
- Some ledges can be reached by jumping.
- The world edge is a soft boundary. You can cross it slightly, but staying out of bounds drains health quickly.

If you see `OUT OF BOUNDS!`, turn back immediately.

## 8. Combat

You do not press a button to attack. Weapons attack automatically based on their cooldowns.

Your combat job is to:

- Keep enemies inside weapon range.
- Avoid taking contact damage.
- Collect XP and gold safely.
- Choose upgrades that work together.
- Use the camera so you can see where danger is coming from.

### Damage and Critical Hits

Most attacks can deal critical hits. A critical hit deals double damage and appears as a larger, brighter damage number.

The player starts with a small critical chance. Some upgrades increase it.

### Knockback

Several attacks push enemies away from the source of damage. Bosses resist knockback more than normal enemies.

### Contact Damage

Enemies hurt you when they touch you on the same ground level. After taking damage, you briefly become invulnerable and flash.

Jumping or standing on higher ground can prevent some contact hits.

## 9. Weapons

You can carry up to four weapons during a run. Each weapon can be upgraded to level 8.

When you level up, a weapon card may:

- Add a new weapon if you do not have it.
- Upgrade an existing weapon by one level.

If you already have four weapons, new weapon cards stop appearing. Existing weapons can still be upgraded.

### Basher Hammer

The Basher Hammer slams the ground around you.

It is best when enemies are close. Upgrades increase damage, radius, and cooldown speed. Hammer also breaks nearby glowing pots.

### Bash Orbs

Bash Orbs circle around the player and damage anything they touch.

They are good for close-range defense. Higher levels add more orbs, increase damage, and improve orbit behavior. Orbs can also block incoming spitter shots.

### Fireball

Fireball shoots at the nearest enemy and explodes on impact.

It is good against groups because the explosion can damage multiple enemies and break pots near the blast.

### Sky Zapper

Sky Zapper calls lightning onto nearby enemies.

It hits random enemies within range. Higher levels strike more targets and deal more damage.

### Boomerang

Boomerang throws a piercing weapon that flies out and returns.

It can hit multiple enemies on the way out and back. Higher levels can add another boomerang and increase damage and range.

### Frost Nova

Frost Nova creates an icy pulse around you.

It damages nearby enemies and slows them by 45 percent for a short time. It is useful when the horde gets too close.

### Cube Cannon

Cube Cannon fires a piercing bolt through the horde.

It is strong in straight lines. At higher levels it can fire a second bolt backward, helping when enemies surround you.

## 10. Passive Level-Up Upgrades

Passive cards improve your hero for the current run only. They do not carry into the next run.

| Passive | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| Big Heart | Adds 25 max health and heals 25 |
| Zoomies | Adds 8 percent move speed |
| Protein Shake | Adds 12 percent damage |
| Caffeine | Reduces weapon cooldowns by 8 percent |
| Gem Magnet | Increases pickup radius by 35 percent |
| Thick Skin | Reduces damage taken by 2 per hit |
| Salad (gross) | Adds 0.8 health regeneration per second |
| Lucky Bash | Adds 6 percent critical chance |
| Afterburner | Adds 12 percent sprint speed |
| Big Lungs | Adds 30 max stamina |
| Second Wind | Adds 35 percent stamina regeneration |

Good first-run passive priorities:

- Big Heart and Thick Skin help you survive mistakes.
- Gem Magnet helps collect XP without walking into danger.
- Caffeine and Protein Shake improve all weapons.
- Zoomies, Afterburner, Big Lungs, and Second Wind help with late-game escapes.

## 11. Enemies

Enemies spawn around the player and chase you. As time passes, enemies spawn faster, more enemies appear per wave, and enemy health and damage increase.

### Grunt

Grunts are the basic cube enemies.

They have moderate health, moderate speed, and basic contact damage. They are common from the beginning.

### Runner

Runners are fast, fragile enemies.

They start appearing after about 40 seconds. They are dangerous because they can catch a walking player, especially later in the run. Sprinting and jumping help against them.

### Brute

Brutes are large, slow, durable enemies.

They start appearing after about 2 minutes. They hit much harder than basic enemies. Do not let them trap you.

### Spitter

Spitters are ranged enemies.

They start appearing after about 6 minutes. They try to stay at range and launch purple shots. You can jump over these shots. Bash Orbs can block them.

### Elite Cube

Elite cubes are stronger special enemies.

After the first minute, an elite appears about every 90 seconds. The game shows an `ELITE CUBE INCOMING` banner. Killing an elite gives extra gems, gold, and a bonus level-up.

### Boss Cube

Bosses are huge enemies with their own health bar.

Bosses appear at:

- 5:00, Megacube
- 10:00, Gigacube
- 15:00, Teracube
- 20:00, Petacube
- 25:00, Omegacube

Bosses are worth fighting because killing one drops a large reward shower, including high-value gems, gold, food, a vacuum pickup, and a bonus level-up.

## 12. Pickups

Pickups appear after enemies die or when pots break.

### XP Gems

XP gems fill the level bar. Different gem colors represent different values.

Collecting enough XP opens the level-up card screen.

### Gold

Gold is collected during the run. At the end of the run, gold is converted into permanent coins.

Coins are used in the Upgrade Lab.

### Hearts

Hearts heal a flat amount of health.

Normal enemies have a small chance to drop hearts.

### Food

Food heals 25 percent of your maximum health.

Food comes from glowing pots and boss rewards.

### Vacuum Pickup

The vacuum pickup pulls all visible pickups toward you. It is very valuable when many XP gems are scattered around the map.

## 13. Glowing Pots

Glowing pots are breakable objects in the world.

Break pots with attacks such as:

- Basher Hammer.
- Fireball explosions.
- Boomerang.
- Bash Orbs.
- Cube Cannon.

When a pot breaks, it drops food. Food heals 25 percent of your maximum health.

Pots respawn after a delay, and pots that are far behind the player relocate back into range.

## 14. Combos

Every enemy kill increases the combo counter. The combo stays active for a short time after each kill.

If you keep killing enemies quickly, the combo grows. Every 50 kills in a combo grants bonus gold.

The combo display appears once the combo reaches 5.

## 15. Upgrade Lab and Permanent Progress

Coins are permanent and saved in the browser. They are stored separately from the current run.

Use coins in the Upgrade Lab to buy permanent upgrades. These upgrades apply at the start of every future run.

| Upgrade | Permanent effect |
| --- | --- |
| Power | Adds 5 percent damage per level |
| Vitality | Adds 10 max health per level |
| Swiftness | Adds 3 percent move speed per level |
| Recovery | Adds 0.15 health regeneration per second per level |
| Endurance | Adds 12 max stamina per level |
| Greed | Adds 10 percent more gold found per level |
| Magnetism | Adds 8 percent pickup radius per level |
| Wisdom | Adds 6 percent XP gained per level |

Each permanent upgrade has a maximum level. Costs increase as you buy more levels.

You can reset all Cube Basher progress from the Upgrade Lab using `reset all progress`. This clears saved coins and permanent upgrades.

## 16. Winning and Losing

### You Win When

The timer reaches 30:00.

You do not need to kill every enemy or defeat every boss to win. Survival is the win condition.

### You Lose When

Your health reaches 0.

Common reasons for losing:

- Standing still while surrounded.
- Running out of stamina at the wrong time.
- Ignoring spitter projectiles.
- Getting trapped by trees, rocks, or ledges.
- Staying out of bounds too long.
- Choosing too many damage upgrades without enough survival upgrades.

## 17. First-Time Player Strategy

For your first few runs:

1. Pick Bonky or Chonk.
2. Keep moving in wide circles.
3. Do not sprint constantly. Save sprint for escapes.
4. Jump when enemies get close.
5. Choose at least one survival passive early.
6. Take Gem Magnet when it appears.
7. Try to build two or three strong weapons before adding a fourth.
8. Break glowing pots when your health is low.
9. When a boss appears, do not tunnel on it. Keep clearing smaller enemies too.
10. Spend coins after every run.

## 18. Advanced Strategy

### Build Around Weapon Roles

A balanced build usually wants:

- One close-range defense weapon, such as Hammer, Orbs, or Frost Nova.
- One ranged or piercing weapon, such as Boomerang, Fireball, Cube Cannon, or Sky Zapper.
- Damage or cooldown passives to scale all attacks.
- Movement or stamina upgrades for late-game survival.

### Control the Horde

Do not run randomly. Try to lead enemies into a long trail behind you. This makes piercing attacks, explosions, and area attacks more effective.

### Manage Stamina

The worst time to become winded is when runners or bosses are close. Release sprint before the stamina bar empties so it can start refilling.

### Use Terrain Carefully

Terrain can save you, but it can also trap you. Jumping up ledges can create space. Running into trees or rocks can end a run.

### Boss Safety

Bosses are dangerous, but the normal horde is often what traps you. Keep moving around the boss instead of backing straight away from it.

## 19. Recommended Beginner Builds

### Safe Learning Build

- Character: Chonk.
- Weapons: Bash Orbs, Frost Nova, Fireball, Cube Cannon.
- Passives: Big Heart, Thick Skin, Gem Magnet, Salad, Caffeine.

This build gives strong defense and steady healing.

### Balanced First Win Build

- Character: Bonky.
- Weapons: Basher Hammer, Boomerang, Sky Zapper, Fireball.
- Passives: Protein Shake, Caffeine, Gem Magnet, Zoomies, Big Heart.

This build mixes close-range safety, ranged damage, and mobility.

### Fast Dodger Build

- Character: Zippy.
- Weapons: Boomerang, Cube Cannon, Frost Nova, Sky Zapper.
- Passives: Zoomies, Big Lungs, Second Wind, Caffeine, Lucky Bash.

This build rewards players who stay moving and avoid direct contact.

## 20. Player Progress in GameHub

Cube Basher reports a progress score back to GameHub at the end of a run. The score is based mostly on survival time, with extra credit for power growth and winning.

A longer run usually means better progress, even if you do not win.

## 21. Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| XP | Experience points from gems. XP fills the level bar. |
| Level-up card | A choice shown when the XP bar fills. |
| Passive | A temporary stat upgrade for the current run. |
| Permanent upgrade | An Upgrade Lab purchase that applies to future runs. |
| Cooldown | The wait time before a weapon can attack again. |
| Crit | A critical hit that deals double damage. |
| Magnet | Pickup radius. A higher magnet pulls gems and items from farther away. |
| Winded | A state where sprint is disabled until stamina refills completely. |
| Elite | A special strong enemy that gives bonus rewards. |
| Boss | A timed major enemy with a health bar and large rewards. |
| Out of bounds | Outside the glowing world edge, where health drains quickly. |
